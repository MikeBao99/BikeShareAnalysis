Note that the data runs from August through March. We can see a steady decrease in trips as the year progresses (starting from August), with ridership increasing again in the following Spring.

We are able to see some interesting trends in this graph. In general, walk-up riders strongly prefer to ride in the afternoons. Meanwhile, we see three peaks for monthly pass riders. This is likely because this cohort contains people who ride bikes to and from work. The peak around 12 PM for this cohort might be from workers who ride bikes to get lunch.

Again, we are able to see the distinction between people who ride bikes for leisure and people who ride bikes to work. Those who ride for leisure seem to go for extended biking trips while those who have a monthly pass probably use it primarily for the 10-15 minute bike ride to and from work.
